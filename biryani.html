Biryani

Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Difficulty: Hard
Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 600g chicken or mutton, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 4 onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons ghee
  • 4 green chilies
  • 1/4 cup ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 black cardamom pods
  • 6-7 green cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon black cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Soak rice: Wash basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate meat: Mix meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Let marinate for 30 minutes.
  3. Fry onions: Heat 2 tablespoons ghee in a pan and fry onions until golden brown and crispy. Remove half for garnish.
  4. Cook meat: Add remaining fried onions to the marinated meat and mix.
  5. Boil rice: In a large pot, boil water with whole spices (cumin seeds, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, cardamom). Add salt and soaked rice. Cook until 70% done, then drain.
  6. Layer (Step 1): In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of partially cooked rice.
  7. Layer (Step 2): Spread the meat mixture evenly over the rice.
  8. Layer (Step 3): Spread the remaining rice on top of the meat.
  9. Garnish: Sprinkle fried onions, fresh mint, and coriander on top. Drizzle remaining ghee.
  10. Dum cook: Cover the pot with aluminum foil, then place the lid. Cook on high heat for 1-2 minutes until steam forms, then cook on low heat for 45 minutes.
  11. Rest: Turn off heat and let it rest for 5 minutes without opening the lid.
  12. Serve: Gently mix the biryani with a fork and serve hot.

Tips

  • Use aged basmati rice for the best aroma and texture.
  • Do not open the lid while the biryani is cooking to maintain the steam.
  • The "dum pukht" (steam cooking) method is crucial for proper biryani texture and flavor.
  • You can use chicken, mutton, goat meat, or even vegetables for different variations.
  • Using a heavy-bottomed pot ensures even cooking and prevents burning at the bottom.
