Shahi Paneer

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 400ml tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup cashew paste
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare: Soak cashews in warm water for 15 minutes, then blend into a smooth paste.
  2. Sauté: Heat ghee in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add paste: Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add spices: Add turmeric and red chili powder, stir well.
  5. Add tomato: Pour tomato puree and cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Add cashew: Add cashew paste and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. Add cream: Pour heavy cream and mix thoroughly. Let it simmer for 3 minutes.
  8. Add paneer: Gently add paneer cubes and cook for 5 minutes without stirring much to avoid breaking them.
  9. Season: Add garam masala and salt. Adjust taste as needed.
  10. Garnish: Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with rice or naan.

Tips

  • Use fresh paneer for the best texture and taste.
  • Cashew paste adds richness to the curry, but you can also use almond paste as a substitute.
  • Don't overcook the paneer as it becomes hard and chewy.
  • Add more cream if you prefer a richer, creamier curry.
